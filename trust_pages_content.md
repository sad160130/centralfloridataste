## PAGE 1 — /methodology/  ("How we grade restaurants")

**The most important thing to know first.**
Florida does not assign letter grades or scores to restaurants. The state's Department of Business and Professional Regulation (DBPR) inspects food establishments and records violations, but it deliberately does not rate or grade them, because each inspection is a snapshot of one moment. **The A–F grades and 0–100 scores on this site are calculated by us from DBPR's public inspection records using the published method below. They are not official Florida grades.** We show our exact method so you can judge it for yourself.

**Where the inspection data comes from.**
We use the DBPR Division of Hotels & Restaurants public-records extracts for District 4, which covers the eight Central Florida counties: Orange, Brevard, Volusia, Seminole, Osceola, Lake, St. Lucie, and Indian River. DBPR refreshes these files regularly. Each record includes the establishment's licensed name and address, every inspection this fiscal year, the disposition of each visit, and the count of violations by severity tier — high-priority, intermediate, and basic.

**How we calculate the score.**
Every restaurant starts at 100 points. From its most recent completed inspection we subtract:

- 8 points per high-priority violation (the items most directly tied to foodborne illness — temperature control, cross-contamination, pest activity, hygiene)
- 3 points per intermediate violation
- 1 point per basic violation
- An additional penalty when the inspection ended in enforcement: a warning, an administrative complaint, or an emergency closure order
- A further deduction if the restaurant had any emergency closure in the trailing year, even if since corrected

The result is clamped to a 0–100 range, and we keep the raw number. Grades follow standard bands: **A is 90+, B is 80–89, C is 70–79, D is 60–69, and F is below 60.** A restaurant with no completed inspection in the current period is marked **Not Rated** rather than given a default grade.

**How we match restaurants to ratings and locations.**
DBPR data has no map coordinates, ratings, or hours, so we match each licensed establishment to its Google Maps listing using its name, address, and location. We verify every match on both location and name agreement, and **we only publish matches we're confident in** — restaurants whose match is uncertain are held back rather than shown with possibly-wrong information. Across our publishable list, about 97% of active establishments had an inspection on file to score.

**How dietary tags are assigned.**
Dietary accommodations (vegan, vegetarian, gluten-free, halal, and so on) come first from a restaurant's own stated offerings on Google, which we treat as the strongest signal. For dimensions that aren't stated outright, we apply an automated classification pass over the restaurant's name and cuisine. These tags indicate that a place *offers options* for a given diet — not that it is exclusively that — and they're a guide, not a guarantee. Always confirm with the restaurant for strict dietary or allergy needs.

**Limitations — read these.**
An inspection is a snapshot; conditions change day to day, and a single report may not reflect a restaurant's long-term operation. Our score is a heuristic built on top of that public data, not a medical or official safety determination. Google ratings and counts shift over time. We refresh from the source on a regular cycle, and each restaurant page shows when its data was last updated. **Found something wrong?** Tell us at [CONTACT EMAIL] and we'll correct it.

## PAGE 2 — /data-sources/  ("Our data & sources")

A short, linkable page listing exactly what we use and where it comes from:

- **Inspection records & licensing** — Florida DBPR, Division of Hotels & Restaurants, public-records extracts (District 4 / Central Florida). The source of every health score, violation, and inspection date.
- **Ratings, location, hours, categories** — Google Maps / Places listings, matched to each licensed establishment.
- **Update cadence** — DBPR data is pulled and re-scored on a regular cycle; the "last updated" date appears on each restaurant page.
- **What we don't do** — we don't accept payment from restaurants to change scores or placement, and the grade is computed purely from the formula on our methodology page.
